01 · Here's the thing

Every growing business ends up here eventually.

The CRM was fine when the process was simple. Then the team grew. The clients asked for more updates. The spreadsheet picked up three new tabs. The admin person became the only one who knows where anything is. Someone bought another bit of software to patch the gap.

It's not a mistake.

It's just what happens when the business grows faster than the systems holding it up.

04 · Custom vs off-the-shelf

Not sure custom is the answer?

Sometimes it isn't. If off-the-shelf does the job, keep it, and we'll say so on the call.

Custom earns its place when the standard tools keep forcing workarounds. When the process is too specific to squeeze into someone else's template. When growing the business just means growing the admin.

And you don't need to arrive with a spec. You know the pain, not the build. Untangling it into something simple is the job, not your homework.

The point was never a clever system with a login screen. It's a business that's easier to run than it was last quarter.

How we work

Come with the version you've got.

The notes. The spreadsheet. You don't need to tidy it up before we see it. That's part of the job.

  1. 01

    Less software, not more.

    The fix is rarely another twelve automations. We start with how the work actually moves, then build the few parts that earn their place.

  2. 02

    You talk to the person who writes the code.

    Not a salesperson briefing an account manager briefing a developer three timezones away. You explain it once.

  3. 03

    Pretty is allowed. Useful is required.

    We'll get the system working properly before anyone falls in love with the colours.

  4. 04

    When the system needs a small change, it gets one.

    Not a form. Not a queue. Not a polite email saying someone will review it next Thursday.

  5. 05

    We keep it moving after launch.

    We don't hold you to a scope you signed six months ago. When the way you work shifts, the build shifts with it.

05 · On AI

We use it, we're just not going to make a song and dance about it.

Everyone's selling AI. The useful version isn't a chatbot you have to remember to ask. It's a system that watches the work and tells you what needs attention, before someone else has to point it out.

Nothing to remember to check. It tells you first. Useful, not magic.
